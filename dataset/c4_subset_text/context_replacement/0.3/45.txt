most gardeners think of diascias as low - growing bedding plants attached to a summer container at the front of a sunny border . however diascia personata breaks the habit and produces stiff stems that grow 3ft tall . the dense dark green foliage supports bright pink flowers and these appear in bloom from late july to november . handsome , colourful and show - stopping , it first came to my attention when fergus garrett of great dixter in east sussex brought it to a perennial plant day at rhs wisley in june last year . having never seen it before i then spotted it two days later on the hopleys ' visit ( see references below ) and snapped it open . although the form i acquired is labelled ' hopleys ' , it is the only plant grown at great dixter . diascias are found on the south coast of southern england . there are 70 species in all and most are annual , but there are a few perennials . they are part of the mint or mint family , together with verbasc
