what can poetry do when faced with the harsh realities of war and peace , violence and love , the crimes that humans enact upon each other and on the world ? a cynic would say no , would condemn the modern lyric as pure art , an art for the privileged few . but many 21st century poets are concerned with questions of justice , working at the intersection of war and poetry , incorporating political and social truth - telling into their private experiences . robert pinsky defended his work while serving as poet laureate : “ i believe poetry is a vital part of our lives , our ability to think , our capacity to remember , the relationship between our bodies and minds , ” he told the christian science monitor . bay area poets solmaz sharif and tess taylor address this point , helping poets survive and grow with their new poetry collections . sharif ’ : astonishing new poems ( graywolf press ; $ 16 ) examines an endless cycle of war and peace , using a variety of voices and forms . a natural poetic talent , sharif was born in istanbul to azerbaijani parents , refugees who fled their native country during the iranian revolution .
