In a culture dominated by Christianity, the Neoplatonic philosophers had to defend the superiority of the Hellenic traditions of wisdom. “ Proclus of Athens” (480 – 485 c.e.) was the foremost philosopher of the last centuries and played a key role in the propagation of the platonic philosophy from antiquity to the Middle Ages.” (Symphony). As an extraordinarily prolific writer, he wrote commentary on Aristotle, Euclid and Plato, systematic treatises in all the fields of philosophy at that time (metaphysics, theology, astronomy, mathematics, ethics), and exegetical works on the traditions of religious wisdom (pneumatic oracles and orphisms). proclus exerted an irresistible influence upon the development of the neoplatonic schools not only in Athens, but also in Alexandria, where his student Ammonius was made the head of the school.
